Socio-Demographic, Labour & Child Vulnerability Profile of Brick Kiln Communities
Baseline Assessment · Migrant Worker Families & Children · Sheikhpura District, Bihar
🏭 40 of 55 brick kilns surveyed 🏠 879 households 👥 4,411 individuals 📍 Sheikhpura, Bihar · 2024
Brick kiln workers belong to India's most marginalized migrant communities. This baseline captures migration patterns, labour conditions, child vulnerability, and welfare exclusion — revealing systemic deprivation across SC/ST migrant kiln populations in Sheikhpura district.

⚠ Overall Vulnerability Analysis
“The data reflects severe educational deprivation, weak financial inclusion, low social protection coverage, and exclusion of migrant children and mothers from education, nutrition, and maternal health systems among marginalized SC/ST brick kiln communities.”

Key Recommendations
Strengthen FLN and bridge education programmes for migrant children
Expand Akshar Learning Centre (ALC) coverage at kiln sites
Improve Anganwadi and POSHAN outreach at migration destinations
Increase birth registration and Aadhaar enrolment for children
Strengthen ANC, postnatal care and maternal nutrition services
Improve BOCW and E-Shram labour welfare registration
Strengthen social protection and welfare scheme access
Mobile health camps and healthcare outreach at brick kilns
Promote safe and regulated migration systems
Strengthen women's SHG / Jeevika participation and financial inclusion
